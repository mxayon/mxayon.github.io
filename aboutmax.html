I grew up in the philippines and named myself after my grandfather. He graduated MIT as an engineer and served in the US military as an engineer. He wrote a paper: "The Economic Feasibility of an Electric Furnace in the Philippines" in the 1940s. A decade before Japan switched from gas furnaces to electric for mass produced steel. Tripling asia's metallurgy economy in the 1950s. As a child I knew my mom had superwoman powers as she juggled interior design projects for shopping centers while teaching me how to read Dr. Seuss. I then lived with my pops who was immersed in his fathers architechture books. My other grandpa was the dean of architechture for the Philippines' architechture college. After WWII in Manila, rebuilding from reparations of the bombs of war, schools and learning were the first things to improve. There was an influx of american book sales men selling whole volumes of encyclopedias and world atlases or history. My dad talked about the books like gold, covering each book to preserve it. He always used these books in every chance he got to teach a lesson. When I was held back in school due to my chicken-scratch handwriting, he told me to re-write this Florence Nightingale biography book in scriptive handwriting. After my father passed, he left behind the small public library, today located at the Barangay Hall in his town. Seeing my older brother relentlessly work on his drafting table till late at night gave me a real idea of what a purposeful profession looks like. These stories, along with the different teachers I was lucky enough to learn from, has structured my interests and resiliency.

The level of tools for exploration today, even after reading new Moore's law re-implementations, is mind blowing in action. Today we are lucky enough to see the effects of each improvement in real-time and we need to be resposible for the repurucssions. Everything goes back to the ideation phase and design in creation. I would love to create design enviroments that champion the science that goes behind it.
